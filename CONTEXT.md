# Simera Trace

Marketing and domain language for Simera Trace’s graphene lubricant programme for mining operations in Africa and the Middle East.

## Client feedback (May 2026) — pending site implementation

Copy changes from **Simera Trace website.docx** are tracked in [docs/website-copy-checklist.md](docs/website-copy-checklist.md). Terms below marked **(proposed)** reflect client direction; live TSX may still use the prior baseline until checklist items are checked off.

**Positioning shift (proposed):** Less “first in Africa / fuel savings trial / 5–8% everywhere”; more **asset performance**, **return on assets**, **company value**, and **split fuel % by vehicle class** (8–13% light vehicles, 3–5% mining/heavy). Validation window **4–6 weeks** (not ~30 days).

## Language

**Fuel savings trial**:
An on-site programme where Simera Trace introduces graphene lubricant into a mine’s lubrication practice and measures fuel and wear outcomes against a baseline.
_Avoid_: Pilot (internal), product purchase, buy online

**Benefits trial** (proposed, May 2026):
Client-preferred variant of the on-site programme emphasising broader operational benefits (fuel, wear, availability), not only diesel %. Use only after replacing “fuel savings trial” consistently across web, email, and PDF.
_Avoid_: Mixing both terms on the same page without intent

**Graphene lubricant platform** (proposed, May 2026):
Executive-facing framing for the combined technology + on-site validation offering (diesel, oil life, availability, engine life, return on assets).
_Avoid_: Implying an off-the-shelf product purchase

**Graphene lubricant**:
A lubricant additive based on graphene that is mixed into existing engine oil during the trial.
_Avoid_: G-Lubricant, GMG, supplier brand names, reseller

**Asset performance** (proposed, May 2026):
How well fleet and plant run—fuel efficiency, wear, uptime, and component life—often used in client hero/banner copy instead of “energy savings” alone.
_Avoid_: Guaranteed outcomes without on-site validation

**Return on assets** / **company value** (proposed, May 2026):
Executive framing: graphene-led savings flow to net profit and enterprise value, not only diesel line items.
_Avoid_: Specific valuation multiples or guaranteed EBITDA impact without validation

**Energy savings**:
The executive-facing benefit of using less fuel and running equipment more efficiently.
_Avoid_: Guaranteed savings, specific % without trial validation context

**Diesel savings** / **fuel savings**:
The measurable reduction in diesel or fuel consumption, usually expressed as a percentage range after on-site validation.

**Public marketing bands (proposed, May 2026):**

- **Light vehicles:** validated **8–13%** fuel savings (client doc)
- **Mining / heavy equipment:** **expected 3–5%** savings (client doc)

**Legacy band (current site + assessment defaults until aligned):** **up to 8%**; **typically 5–8%** after on-site validation. See [docs/assessment-output-guide.md](docs/assessment-output-guide.md) and the checklist for divergence.

_Avoid_: Guaranteed, universal, one-size-fits-all claims; using the old single 5–8% band on pages that have moved to split bands

**Cost per tonne-kilometre (cost/t-km)**:
Total haulage operating cost divided by tonne-kilometres moved; the primary executive efficiency metric.
_Avoid_: Fuel-only metric, partial OPEX proxy

**Indicative assessment**:
A self-serve estimate generated from submitted mine inputs; not yet site-validated.
_Avoid_: Final result, guaranteed outcome

**Savings estimate**:
The assessment output range that combines fuel-related and financial impact projections.
_Avoid_: Fixed saving, guaranteed percentage

**Haulage cost**:
The annual cost of running the haul-truck fleet, including fuel, tyres, maintenance, labour, and depreciation.
_Avoid_: Fuel cost only

**Cost/t-km improvement**:
The percentage reduction in cost per tonne-kilometre from lubrication-led efficiency gains.
_Avoid_: Guaranteed universal improvement

**Benefit mechanism**:
A measurable pathway linking lubrication behavior to lower cost/t-km, such as reduced friction, lower wear, improved uptime, or extended oil life.
_Avoid_: Marketing claim without measurable signal

**Tribochemical action** (proposed, May 2026):
Mechanism copy: graphene platelets fill crevices and asperities, “reconditioning” metal surfaces from the inside; used in wear/engine-life messaging.
_Avoid_: Publishing unreviewed technical claims (e.g. specific friction/wear % in client doc) without engineering sign-off

**Validation protocol**:
The phased on-site measurement process used to confirm assessment outputs with mine data.
**Typical window (proposed):** **4–6 weeks** on the operation (replaces “~30 days” in earlier copy).
_Avoid_: One-off estimate, desk-only analysis

**ESG benefits** (proposed, May 2026 — FAQ):
Client-supplied figures for marketing FAQ only until validated: e.g. **27% NOx**, **~55 t CO₂ per haul truck per year**, reductions in **PM** and **HC**, less oil waste, extended component life.
_Avoid_: Publishing in assessment PDF/email without legal/engineering review

**Mine**:
The primary customer operation—open-pit or underground mining with heavy diesel-powered equipment.
_Avoid_: Customer (too generic), client (prefer mine or operation)

**Maintenance team**:
People responsible for lubrication, equipment health, and uptime—including tribologists and reliability engineers.
_Avoid_: User, account

## Relationships

- A **Fuel savings trial** (or **Benefits trial**, if adopted) applies **Graphene lubricant** at a **Mine** and produces evidence of **Energy savings** / **Asset performance** and reduced wear
- **Energy savings** is the umbrella term; **Diesel savings** is the quantified subset used in stats and assessments
- **Return on assets** / **company value** tie executive narrative to **cost/t-km** and financial assessment outputs

## Example dialogue

> **Exec:** "What do we actually get from Simera Trace?"
> **Domain expert (proposed, May 2026):** "We help you prove how **graphene lubricant** improves **asset performance** on your mine—diesel, wear, and uptime—against your baseline. You get indicative numbers from the assessment, then **validation on site** in about **4–6 weeks** so leadership can see impact on **return on assets**, not generic promises."

> **Domain expert (legacy baseline, until copy updated):** "An on-site **fuel savings trial**. We introduce **graphene lubricant** into your existing oil programme, measure diesel burn and wear indicators, and you see whether **energy savings** and longer component life stack up on your fleet—not on paper."

## Flagged ambiguities

- "Fuel optimisation" and "energy savings" both appear in copy—resolved: **energy savings** in headlines; **diesel / fuel %** in stats and assessment outputs. **(May 2026):** client also pushes **asset performance** / **return on assets** in hero—may supersede dual-pillar “energy + equipment life” on homepage.
- Third-party product names must never appear on public pages—resolved: use **graphene lubricant** only.
- "Diesel savings %" and "cost/t-km improvement %" can be conflated—resolved: report both, with diesel savings for operations and cost/t-km for executive financial framing.
- Client source files use G® / GMG naming—resolved for this product: use **Simera Trace / graphene lubricant** language in web, email, and PDF outputs.
- **Single 5–8% band vs split bands (May 2026):** marketing checklist uses 8–13% / 3–5%; assessment model and email may still use 5–8% until a dedicated alignment pass—see checklist “Downstream”.
- **Fuel savings trial vs benefits trial / platform (May 2026):** pick one programme name for public surfaces or define when each applies.
- **First in Africa vs Pioneering the benefits of graphene in Africa (May 2026):** both Africa-first; confirm final badge line with client.
- **UI purple vs navy (May 2026):** client asked to replace purple UI with navy; **brand logo** remains purple asset in header—see checklist D1 vs D2.
- **Footer ESG CTA (May 2026):** “download the complete estimate”—target URL not specified (assessment PDF? contact?).
- **Tribochemical / science % (May 2026):** client doc adds 50% friction, 20–70% wear, PoS 30%—needs technical review before publish.
